Career

Agrawal held research internships at Research
and Yahoo! Research prior to joining Twitter
as a software engineer in 2011.[12] In October
2017, Twitter announced the appointment of Agrawal
as chief technology officer following the departure
of Adam Messinger.[13] In December 2019, Twitter CEO Jack Dorsey
announced that Agrawal would be in charge of Project Bluesky
, an initiative to develop a decentralized social
network protocol.[14] In November 2021, Dorsey announced
that he was resigning as CEO of Twitter and that Agrawal
was replacing him immediately.[15][16] As CEO, Agrawal was
awarded annual compensation of $1 million as well as stock
compensation worth $12.5 million.[7]Views and policies
